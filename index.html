About me

When I was still in high-school, my father bought my first computer, a Commodore 64. We actually took a programming class together, but that was back in the old DOS days and it didn't really amount to anything. But when Windows 95 was launched, my interest in computers and programming really got started. After a few years, I signed up for a lifetime membership at learnvisualstudio.com, following the video lectures of Bob Tabor from the USA. He also has a series on Javascript and that's how I got into web design. After coding nearly every day since then, I realized that I wanted to formalize my knowledge. A friend of mine suggested Lynda.com (now LinkedIn Learning) where I've taken a number of courses related to programming and web-design. Later I discovered the Noroff University, based in Norway, where I'm currently studying Frontend Development.
